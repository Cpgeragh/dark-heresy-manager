12789: — Ancient (pre-Heresy) Proverb.
12790: Weapons are divided into several groups covering
12791: a plethora of ranged weapons, dealing with
12792: everything from crossbows to plasma cannons, and
12793: melee weapons covering all manner of close combat weaponry.
12794: Unless they are of Poor quality, all weapons come with the
12795: appropriate holster or carrying strap as part of their cost.
12796: All weapons have a profile that is presented using the
12797: following statistics, providing players and GMs with all the
12798: relevant information needed to use them:
12799: Name: What the weapon is called.
12800: Class: Describes what class the weapon is, either a melee,
12801: thrown, pistol, basic or heavy weapon.
12802: • Melee weapons can be used in close combat. When using a
12803: melee weapon you add your SB to the damage you inflict.
12804: • Thrown weapons cover anything propelled by muscle power
12805: alone and covers knives, axes, spears and such like. Unless they
12806: are also classed as melee, thrown weapons cannot be used in close
12807: combat (or if they do, they count as improvised weapons). When
12808: using a thrown weapon you add your SB to the damage you inflict
12809: (with the exception of explosives such as grenades).
12810: • Pistol weapons are fired one-handed and can be used
12811: in close combat. However, when a pistol is used in close
12812: combat, the firer gains no bonuses or penalties to hit for
12813: range or targeting equipment.
12814: • Basic weapons normally require two hands but can be
12815: used one-handed with a –20 penalty to hit.
12816: •Heavy weapons always require two hands and must be braced
12817: in some way, usually either on a bipod or tripod but equally
12818: it could be a windowsill or sandbag (or the firer’s shoulder
12819: in the case of RPG launchers), to be fired without penalties.
12820: Firing a heavy weapon without bracing incurs a –30 penalty
12821: to hit and prohibits Semi-Auto and Full Auto fire.
12822: Range: This number is used to determine how far the
12823: weapon can be accurately fired in metres. A weapon’s Short
12824: Range is half this number while its Long Range is double this
12825: number (for ranged bonuses and penalties see Chapter VII:
12826: Playing the Game, pages 198-199). Weapons cannot be
12827: fired at targets more than four times their range distance.
12828: RoF (Rate of Fire): Tells you if the weapon can be fired
12829: either on Semi-Auto or Full Auto and how many rounds/
12830: charges it expends when doing so. A weapon’s RoF has three
12831: entries to indicate the modes a weapon can be fired in. The
12832: first entry indicates whether the weapon can be fired singly
12833: (with an S). The second entry indicates whether or not a
12834: weapon can be fired semi-automatically, and the number listed
12835: describes the shots fired. Finally, the third entry describes if
12836: the weapon can be fired on full automatic. Any mode in which
12837: the weapon cannot be fired is indicated with a “—”.
12838: Some weapons can be fired in more than one mode. If this
12839: is the case each of its different rates of fire will be listed in its
12840: profile. Characters must choose which mode they are firing
12841: their weapon in before making their attack.
12842: Dam (Damage): The Damage the weapon does and the type
12843: of damage it inflicts for the purpose of Criticals (as shown in

12900: Some weapons possess special qualities to represent such
12901: things as special damage or unusual effects.
12902: Accurate
12903: Some weapons are designed with precision in mind and respond
12904: superbly in skilled hands. They grant an additional bonus of
12905: +10 to the firer’s Ballistic Skill when used with an Aim Action,
12906: this is in addition to the bonus granted from Aiming.
12907: Balanced
12908: Some weapons, such as swords and knives, are designed so that
12909: the weight of the hilt balances the weight of the blade, making
12910: the weapon easier to wield. Balanced weapons grant a +10
12911: bonus to Weapon Skill Tests made to Parry.
12912: Blast (X)
12913: Many missiles, grenades and some guns create an explosion when
12914: they hit their target. When working out a hit from a Blast weapon
12915: anyone within the weapon’s blast radius in metres, indicated by
12916: the number in parenthesis, is also hit. Roll Hit Location and
12917: Damage individually for each person affected by a blast.
12918: Defensive
12919: A Defensive weapon, such as a shield, is intended to be used
12920: to block attacks and is awkward when used for making attacks.
12921: Defensive weapons grant a +15 bonus to tests made when used
12922: to Parry, but take a –10 penalty when used to make attacks.
12923: Flame
12924: Flame weapons project a cone of flame out to the range of the
12925: weapon. Unlike other weapons, flamers have just one range, and
12926: when fired, cast fiery death out to this distance. The wielder
12927: does not need to Test Ballistic Skill; he simply fires the weapon.
12928: All creatures in the flame’s path, a cone-shaped area extending
12929: in a 30 degree arc from the firer out to the weapon’s range,
12930: must make an Agility Test or be struck by the flames and take
12931: damage normally. If they take damage, they must succeed on
12932: a second Agility Test or be set on fire. Cover does not protect
12933: characters from attacks made by Flame weapons.
12934: Because Flame weapons make no roll to hit, they are always
12935: considered to hit targets in the body, and will Jam if the firer
12936: rolls a 9 on his Damage dice (before adding any bonuses).
12937: Flexible
12938: Some weapons are made up from lots of loosely connected
12939: segments, such as chains or supple woven hides, such as whips.
12940: These kinds of weapons lash about when used to attack and
12941: cannot be Parried.
12942: Inaccurate
12943: Weapons with this quality are either badly designed or simply
12944: woefully made, and regardless of the care taken when used,
12945: offer little better than a lucky chance to hit. No bonus is gained
12946: froom the use of the Aim Action with such weapons.
